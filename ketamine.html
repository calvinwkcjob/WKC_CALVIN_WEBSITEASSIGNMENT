Ketamine

Type:

Ketamine Hydrochloride is a commercially produced general anaesthetic used mainly for sedating large animals. It is produced as an injectable clear colourless liquid which through evaporation can be converted to a white crystalline powder similar to cocaine hydrochloride. Abusers may experience an "out of body" or "near death" feeling. In Hong Kong, Ketamine mainly appears in powder form and the most common packaging method is by wrapping in a small piece of paper or a banknote.

Effects:
Abusers may suffer from delirium, impaired memory, impaired motor function, respiratory / heart problems / dependence after long-term consumption.
